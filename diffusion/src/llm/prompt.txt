[Task overview & Goal]:

You are given a JSON file representing a two-hand 3D motion sequence. Your goal is to generate a clear, structured, and concise paragraph summarizing the physical motion of both hands, based on the provided JSON-formatted motion data. Focus strictly on joint states, finger movement, and inter-hand spatial relationships — without interpreting gesture meaning or intent.

[Input Format & Structure Description]:

You are given a JSON file representing a 3D two-hand motion sequence. The structure is composed of three top-level keys:

- frame_count
- left_hand_events
- right_hand_events
- two_hand_relationships

Each section includes multiple events, organized by type. An event is recorded as a list of dictionaries, each representing a meaningful temporal change. Each event dictionary includes:

- start: Start frame index of the event
- end: End frame index of the event
- start_des: Description of the state at the start
- end_des: Description of the state at the end
- constant_des: Describes a consistently maintained state throughout the interval without significant change
- v_des: Description of movement speed (e.g., Slow, Medium, Fast)
- direction: (only present in wrist trajectory) Direction of movement over time

Hand Event Types:
Under left_hand_events and right_hand_events, the following categories are defined:

1. finger_flexing
    - Keyed by finger joints (e.g., index_mcp, thumb_ip)
    - Describes flexion transitions: bending/straightening
2. finger_spacing
    - Keyed by adjacent finger pairs (e.g., "index, middle")
    - Captures dynamic spacing between adjacent fingers.
3. finger_distance
    - Keyed by fingertip pairs (e.g., "thumb_tip, index_tip")
    - Describes the contact condition between the thumb and the fingertips of the other four fingers on the same hand.
4. wrist_trajectory
    - Axes: left-right, front-back, down-up
    - Describes the wrist's motion along vertical, horizontal, and depth axes.

Two-Hand Relationships:
The two_hand_relationships section captures inter-hand spatial dynamics:

1. finger_finger_distance
    - Fingertip-to-fingertip distances across hands
2. finger_palm_distance
    - Fingertip-to-opposite-palm distances across hands
3. palm_palm_distance
    - Scalar distance between palms over time
4. palm_palm_relative_position
    - Axial description of spatial positioning between palms
    - Includes: left-right, front-back, up-down

[Task Instruction]**:**

Analyze the full motion sequence and generate a single, well-structured paragraph that **summarizes the motion over time**. Focus strictly on the physical aspects: finger movements, wrist motion, and inter-hand distances or alignment. Do **not** interpret gestures, infer intent, or provide symbolic meanings. Your output must adhere to the following constraints:

- Output one paragraph only — no lists, bullet points, explanations, or any extra formatting
- Focus solely on motion: do **not** infer gestures, intent, meaning, or symbolic behavior.
- Use direct, confident, and unambiguous language.
- Always describe finger flexing at the level of the whole finger. Do not break it down into joint-level details. Exception: If a finger shows hyperextension at the base joint but bending at the distal joint, you should describe the joints separately and clearly.
- If there are events such as contact or hyperextension, make sure they are always included in the annotation and never omitted.
- Indicate the temporal position of an event within the sequence. For example, whether it appears at the start, the end, or recurs across the sequence.

[Motion Description Rules (with Examples)]:

1. **Structure:**
    - Begin with the left hand's motion.
    - Then describe the right hand.
    - End with inter-hand spatial relationships.
2. **Left/Right Hand Motion Guidelines:**
    - Highlight notable features: extreme state change, contact, high-speed changes, significant spacing shifts, extreme flexion.
        - Good: The left index finger quickly moves from fully extended to fully bent. (extrame flexion state change, high-speed motion)
        - Good: The spacing between the right index and middle fingers changes from a touching state to being far apart at a medium speed. (extrame flexion state change)
        - Good: The fingertips of the left pinky and thumb maintain contact. (Contact information is important)
        - Bad: The right thumbtip and ring fingertip maintain a certain distance from each other. (There is no extreme state or high-speed change; separation between the thumb and ring finger is common.)
    - Summarize flexion at the finger level only: Do **not** describe individual joints; Combine joint data to describe **each finger as a whole** in terms of its overall bend state over time.
        - Good: The left index finger remains in a fully bent state.
        - Bad:  The MCP joint of the left index finger remains in a fully bent state; the PIP joint of the left index finger remains in a fully bent state; the DIP joint of the left index finger remains in a fully bent state.
    - Avoid trivial spatial details (e.g., natural finger adjacency) unless extreme or unique.
        - Bad: The index and middle fingers are near each other.
    - Condense similar finger behaviors for clarity and conciseness.
        - Good: The right index and middle fingers move from partially bent to fully extended.
        - Bad:  The right index finger move from partially bent to fully extended; The right middlefinger move from partially bent to fully extended
    - Summarize the repeated pattern of finger/hand motion.
        - Good: The left index finger and the right palm repeatedly make contact and then move apart.
        - Bad: he left index finger and the right palm make contact, move apart, make contact, move apart, and then make contact.
3. **Two-Hand Relationship Guidelines:**
    - Emphasize meaningful interactions and features: contact, high speed motion, extreme state change
        - Good: The right index fingertip quickly moved from a distant position to make contact with the left palm. (contact, high speed motion)
        - Good: Initially, the right hand was positioned above the left hand, but it gradually moved to a position below it. (extreme state change)
        - Bad: The left ring fingertip and the right thumb fingertip maintained a relatively large distance. (no meaningful interaction and feature)
    - Avoid trivial spatial details unless extreme or unique. (Similar to single hand motion guideline)
    - Condense similar finger behaviors for clarity and conciseness. (Similar to single hand motion guideline)
4. **Contact Extra Rule:**
    - If a fingertip makes contact with another part (e.g., a palm or another fingertip), do not describe additional proximity or distance relations involving that fingertip. Contact overrides all other spatial relationships.
        - Good: The left index fingertip made contact with the right palm center. （ No need to also mention 'the fingertip approached the palm' or 'distance reduced'.)
        - Bad: The left index fingertip moved closer to the right palm and made contact, while staying near the right middle finger. (Redundant:  no need to mention proximity to the right middle finger.)

[Style]**:**

- Maintain clarity and fluency; Avoid speculation (e.g., “might,” “seems,” “possibly”);
- Summarize only the motion — do not include explanations or metadata.
- Avoid repetition. Prioritize precision and tempo in the narrative.
